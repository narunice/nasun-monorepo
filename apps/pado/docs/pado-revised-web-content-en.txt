--- 1. Introduction ---

Pado is a high-performance, self-custodial financial platform that unifies
trading, lending, payments, and contextual finance into a single onchain
account. Built on Nasun — an object-oriented, Move-based Layer 1 optimized
for parallel execution — Pado delivers exchange-grade performance while
preserving full user control, transparency, and deterministic risk enforcement.

Instead of forcing users to fragment capital and decision-making across
multiple protocols, wallets, and external social channels, Pado introduces
one smart account governed by a unified risk engine — where capital earns
yield, backs trades, remains instantly usable across products, and operates
within a shared financial context.


--- 2. Why Pado Exists ---

Modern DeFi is powerful — but fragmented.

  * Fragmented Capital
    Users manage multiple wallets and lock capital into isolated pools for
    trading, lending, or payments — reducing capital efficiency and increasing
    operational risk.

  * Protocol Sprawl
    Each DeFi vertical — DEXs, perpetuals, lending protocols, staking —
    operates in isolation, forcing users to bridge liquidity and accept
    inconsistent custody assumptions.

  * Context Switching
    Strategy discussion, market analysis, and coordination happen off-platform
    — on X, Discord, and Telegram — far removed from execution. By the time
    insight becomes action, the opportunity is often gone.

  * No Shared Financial Context
    Existing platforms separate execution from information. Users constantly
    switch between trading interfaces and external channels to learn,
    coordinate, and compete.

Pado consolidates these functions into a single onchain financial system —
allowing users to trade, lend, borrow, pay, and interact from one account
with shared collateral, deterministic risk management, and embedded
financial context.


--- 3. The Core Innovation ---

One Account, One Risk Engine

At the center of Pado is a Smart Account Object that represents a user's
complete financial state.

  * Portfolio-Level Risk
    All assets, positions, and liabilities are evaluated at the portfolio
    level — not per protocol, not per product.

  * Deterministic Enforcement
    Risk is enforced deterministically onchain — no discretionary
    liquidations, no hidden rules. The rules are the same for everyone.

  * Yield on Collateral
    Capital is designed to earn yield while simultaneously backing leveraged
    positions — enabling capital efficiency previously only available in
    centralized exchanges.

This architecture delivers centralized-exchange capital efficiency —
without custody, opaque controls, or fragmented workflows.


--- 4. Built for Performance at Scale ---

Pado leverages Nasun's object-oriented execution model to eliminate global
state bottlenecks.

  * Parallel Execution
    Independent assets and accounts process simultaneously — high throughput
    across multiple markets without network-wide contention.

  * Atomic Transactions
    Multi-step strategies execute as one guaranteed outcome. Supply collateral,
    open positions, and transfer funds — all at once, all or nothing.

  * Sub-Second Finality
    Exchange-grade responsiveness for active trading.

Assets, positions, and markets are modeled as programmable objects, allowing
Pado to scale across products and users without sacrificing safety or
composability.

  Safe Mode [Default] — Live

    - No leverage
    - Ideal for new users

  Pro Mode [Opt-In] — Planned

All modes are enforced by the same onchain risk engine, ensuring fairness,

--- 7. Contextual Finance: The Social Layer for Serious Capital ---

Finance is inherently social — traders compare performance, share strategies,
learn from peers, and compete. Today, these interactions happen outside the
execution layer, across disconnected social platforms with no shared context,
no verification, and no safety rails.

Pado inverts this model. Instead of injecting financial mechanics into social
feeds, Pado embeds optional, high-signal social surfaces directly into the
financial execution layer — eliminating context switching while preserving
privacy and focus.

Design Principle: Social features must make users better traders, better
allocators, or better learners. If they don't, they don't ship.

Modes, Not Feeds:

  * War Rooms [Roadmap]
    Position-gated discussion spaces (e.g. ETH longs, yield strategies)
    designed to filter noise through skin-in-the-game participation.

  * Privacy-First Leaderboards [Roadmap]
    Risk-adjusted, ZKP-verified performance rankings — anonymous by default,
    opt-in public exposure. Designed to reward discipline over speculation.

  * Secure Messaging [Roadmap]
    Encrypted, self-custodial DMs and strategy sharing with built-in risk
    controls. No forced exposure, no open spam channels.

  * AI Agents as Financial Tools [Roadmap]
    Clearly labeled AI agents operate within strict permissions to add
    context — not distraction:
      - Risk alerts and margin health monitoring
      - Market explanations and volatility summaries
      - Strategy simulations before execution
      - Guided onboarding for new users

Live today: global chat with signature-based authentication, integrated
directly into the trading interface.

Social features scale with risk modes — educational in Safe Mode,
performance-oriented in Pro Mode, and private, team-based in Institutional
Mode. Pado becomes not just where capital is deployed — but where financial
decisions are made.


--- 8. Intent-Based & Agent-Ready ---

Designed for the Agentic Future

Pado introduces an intent-based execution layer that allows users (or
autonomous agents) to express financial goals rather than manual transactions.

Example: "Maintain a 5% yield on my stablecoins while keeping my ETH
position above a 1.6x health factor."

  * Solver Network
    A decentralized solver network designed to compete to fulfill intents,
    executing them atomically onchain.

  * Atomic Settlement
    If the outcome doesn't meet the user's signed intent, the transaction
    fails — protecting users from partial execution and slippage.

  * Scoped Permissions
    Users can issue limited-power keys to autonomous agents — restricted by
    risk guardrails, with no custody or unrestricted access.


--- 9. Secure Asset Bridging ---

Pado is designed to support external assets via Nasun's native bridge
infrastructure:

  - Assets locked on source chains via audited smart contracts
  - 1:1 Nasun-native representations issued as onchain objects
  - Secured by the same validator set that secures the network

All bridge logic will be open-source and auditable, with balances and
issuance verifiable onchain.


--- 10. Economic Model ---

  - Competitive trading fees with volume-based discounts
  - Protocol-sponsored gas for core financial actions
  - Sustainable fee structure designed to scale with usage

An onchain insurance fund absorbs protocol-level risk during extreme market
events — protecting users from systemic failures while preserving individual
accountability.
