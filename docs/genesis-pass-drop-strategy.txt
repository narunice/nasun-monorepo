========================================================================
RESEARCH SUMMARY: LESSONS FROM OTHER PROJECTS
========================================================================

FAILURE PATTERNS
----------------

1. The 2024 NFT Massacre: 98% Failure Rate
   - 98% of 2024 NFT drops failed to sustain even 10 trades in their first week
   - NFT supply surged to 1.34 billion while total sales dropped 37%
   - Lesson: Oversupply kills. Per-wallet limits and time-gating are essential.

2. The Open Edition Trap
   - Time-limited unlimited minting was trendy, but most became "Ghost NFTs"
     by 2025: high supply, zero active buyers
   - Root cause: unlimited per-wallet minting + speculation-driven demand
   - Lesson: Open editions fail when whales can mint hundreds. Per-wallet
     limits fundamentally change the dynamic.

3. Trust Erosion
   - Rug pulls drained $3.4B from investors
   - Unfulfilled roadmaps were the #1 trust destroyer
   - Lesson: Promise small, deliver big. No overpromising.

SUCCESS PATTERNS
----------------

1. Pudgy Penguins: Low Price + Scarcity = Explosive Growth
   - 0.03 ETH (~$60 at the time), 8,888 units, sold out in 20 minutes
   - Inverted the typical strategy: mainstream adoption first, then exclusive community
   - Lesson: Low entry barrier + genuine scarcity creates organic viral growth.

2. Heroic Story: Genesis Pass as a Funnel
   - 1,049 free mints, followed by stealth airdrop to holders
   - Holders organically shared on Twitter because their self-interest aligned
     with promotion
   - Lesson: Make the Genesis Pass a gateway to future value.
     Holders become evangelists.

3. PSG (Paris Saint-Germain): 2-Phase Mint Pass
   - Phase 1: Silver Card Pledge mint
   - Phase 2: Only Phase 1 holders get access to limited edition
   - Lesson: Layered drops that reward existing holders while expanding reach.

4. Time-Gated Open Editions (2024-2025 trend)
   - 24-hour mints create high urgency and usually result in lower supply
     and higher secondary floor prices
   - Shorter windows = stronger "founding member" identity
   - Lesson: Time-based scarcity works when combined with per-wallet limits.


========================================================================
STRATEGY: TIME-GATED 3-PHASE DROP
========================================================================

Core Concept: No supply cap. Scarcity comes from TIME, not quantity.
Once the mint window closes, no new Genesis Passes can ever be minted.
Combined with 1-per-wallet enforcement, the final supply organically
reflects the true size of Nasun's early community.

Narrative: "This NFT is only for founding members. If you weren't here
in the first 5 days, you can never get one."

========================================================================
WHY THIS STRUCTURE WORKS
========================================================================

1. Time-Based Scarcity (vs. Supply-Based)
   - No supply cap means no one gets locked out during the mint window
   - But the hard deadline creates genuine urgency: "now or never"
   - Final supply = organic community size, not an arbitrary number
   - After close, the only way to get one is secondary market

2. Open Edition Failure Modes Are Neutralized
   - Classic open edition failures: whales mint 100+, flip, community collapses
   - Here: 1 per wallet, on-chain enforced. No whale accumulation possible.
   - Each NFT represents one real person, not speculative inventory
   - This is a membership pass, not a tradeable art piece

3. "Founding Member" Identity
   - "I was there in the first 5 days" is a powerful community identity
   - Unlike supply-capped drops, no one who wanted one was shut out
   - The narrative is inclusive during the window, exclusive after it closes
   - Holders have pride of ownership tied to timing, not luck or speed

4. Battalion Funnel (Heroic Story / PSG Model)
   - Genesis Pass is not a standalone product. It's the entry ticket to
     the Battalion NFT ecosystem.
   - "Buy Genesis now, get Battalion benefits later" drives Genesis demand
   - Genesis holders become natural promoters: their self-interest aligns
     with project growth
   - Narrative arc: Genesis (founding) -> Battalion (expansion)

5. Price Escalation Creates Urgency
   - Free -> $10 -> $15 -> Battalion (higher price)
   - Earlier participation = lower cost, always
   - The $5 gap between Allowlist and Public is small enough to not exclude
     anyone, but large enough to make Allowlist membership feel rewarded

6. Revenue Projection (Bootstrapped-Friendly)

   Optimistic:    (100 x $0) + (370 x $10) + (500 x $15) = $11,200
   Realistic:     (100 x $0) + (300 x $10) + (200 x $15) = $6,000
   Conservative:  (100 x $0) + (200 x $10) + (80 x $15)  = $3,200

   Revenue is a secondary goal.
   The primary goal is onboarding and community growth before Battalion.

========================================================================
WHY NOT SUPPLY-CAPPED?
========================================================================

Supply-capped (e.g. 1,000 units) was considered but rejected because:

1. It creates frustration: "I wanted one but it sold out"
   - Bad first impression for potential community members
   - Especially risky during Public Mint when new users discover Nasun

2. It's an arbitrary number: why 1,000? Why not 800 or 1,200?
   - Time-based closing is cleaner: "you had 5 days"
   - The final number is organic and honest

3. It doesn't match the goal:
   - Goal is maximum onboarding before Battalion
   - A supply cap limits onboarding by definition
   - Time cap allows maximum onboarding within a defined window

4. 1-per-wallet already prevents the real problem:
   - The danger of uncapped supply is whale accumulation
   - With 1-per-wallet, uncapped supply just means "more real members"


========================================================================
KEY RISKS AND MITIGATIONS
========================================================================

Risk: Very low mint count (looks like low demand)
  -> Mitigate: Don't announce expected numbers beforehand.
     Frame any count as "our founding community."
     Even 300 founding members is a strong start for a bootstrapped L1.

Risk: Sybil attacks (one person, many wallets)
  -> Mitigate: At $10-15 per mint + gas, the economic cost of sybil
     is self-limiting. Each fake wallet costs real money.
     Optional: require leaderboard registration or X account link.

Risk: Genesis holders don't engage after minting
  -> Mitigate: Holder-exclusive events in Week 2-3.
     Battalion benefits announcement keeps them invested.

Risk: Secondary market price crashes
  -> Mitigate: 1-per-wallet means low circulating supply relative
     to holders. Most people hold membership passes, not flip them.
     The Battalion utility gives ongoing reason to hold.

Risk: Public Mint attracts zero interest
  -> Mitigate: Coordinate social push during Day 3-5.
     Existing holders sharing their NFT creates organic reach.
