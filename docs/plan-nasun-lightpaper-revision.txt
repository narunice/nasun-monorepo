Engagement Architecture (Live on Devnet)

Daily Mission System: 5-6 lightweight activities, each under a minute, each awarding
ecosystem points. Claim tokens from the faucet (+1). Send tokens to another wallet (+1).
Buy a lottery ticket (+1). Play a scratch card (+1). Play number match (+1). When a
governance proposal is active, voting replaces one mission slot (+1). A sixth mission,
spot trading on Pado (+2), activates at public testnet. Missions reset at UTC midnight
and are verified directly from on-chain transaction data, not self-reported.

NFT Multiplier Stacking: Ecosystem points are calculated as (base score x multiplier)
+ bonus points. The multiplier is determined by which NFTs a user has activated:

  Alliance NFT (free):     1.0x base multiplier
  Genesis Pass (paid):     2.0x base multiplier (highest base tier wins)
  Battalion (paid):        +5.0x per unit held, up to 10 units

  Maximum multiplier: 20x (e.g., Genesis Pass 2x + 3 Battalion = 17x)

This creates behavioral lock-in: users who accumulate NFTs have a compounding incentive
to maintain daily activity. Stopping means their multiplier advantage produces nothing.
This is the post-incentive retention signal. Stickiness is tied to owned on-chain assets,
not temporary token rewards.

Health System (7-day rolling window): The health gauge displays active days out of seven
as a circular donut (e.g., 5/7). Users who miss two or more days in any seven-day window
enter "Weakened" state, pausing their multiplier until activity resumes. Genesis Pass
holders are immune via "Genesis Shield," always showing 100% health. The health mechanic
creates streak pressure similar to Duolingo's retention model, but tied to financial
activity rather than content consumption.

Genesis Pass: Eight lore-themed editions (Colony, Dawn, Revolt, Echo, Awakening,
Emergence, Rebirth, War) minted on Ethereum. Holders receive 2x base multiplier, health
immunity, and 90% of airdrop allocation is reserved for Genesis Pass holders. The pass
creates a two-tier community: casual participants who earn at 1x, and committed
participants who signal conviction through NFT ownership and earn at 2x or higher.

Points are non-transferable reputation signals, not tokens. They cannot be farmed and
dumped. They gate future benefits including airdrops, governance weight, and access tiers.
This is a deliberate design choice grounded in the behavioral research that informs the
entire engagement architecture: durable identity requires non-fungible commitment.

### 3. Section 5.4 (Baram) - Add AER Technical Detail

Add after "Every settlement produces exactly one AER...":

---

AER Structure: 8 categories, 31 fields per report:

  1. WHO Requester (3 fields): initiator address, authorizer, delegation path (up to
     5-deep agent chains)
  2. WHO Executor (2 fields): executor address, executor principal
  3. HOW MUCH (6 fields): payment amount, payment token, executor received, fee detail,
     budget ID, budget remaining
  4. WHAT (5 fields): model name, model metadata, input hash (SHA-256), output hash
     (SHA-256), execution time in milliseconds
  5. WHY (3 fields): declared purpose, policy version, constraints
  6. HOW TRUSTWORTHY (5 fields): executor tier (Open/Bronze/Silver/Gold), reputation
     score (0-1000), stake amount, TEE verification status, attestation hash
  7. WHEN (3 fields): request timestamp, settlement timestamp, status
  8. CHAIN (2 fields): triggering agent, triggered action (for multi-agent workflows)

Each AER is created via Move's hot-potato receipt pattern: a settlement receipt object
MUST be consumed by the AER module before the transaction completes. The linear type
system enforces this at compile time. There is no code path that settles a request
without producing an audit trail.

Budget delegation system: Before an AI agent spends on behalf of a human principal, the
principal defines constraints on-chain: maximum per-request spend, daily/weekly/monthly
caps, allowed models, allowed executors, category-based limits, and hard expiration
timestamp. Rate limiting is enforced on-chain, preventing runaway agent spending.

Executor accountability: four-tier system based on both stake and reputation (tier equals
the minimum of stake tier and reputation tier, so both dimensions must qualify):

  Open:   no requirements, available to all
  Bronze: 1,000 NASUN staked + 300/1000 reputation
  Silver: 5,000 NASUN staked + 500/1000 reputation
  Gold:   10,000 NASUN staked + 700/1000 reputation

Staking includes 7-day unbonding and graduated slashing: 5% for timeout, 10% for
attestation failure, 100% for fraud. Reputation starts at 500 and decays 50 points per
30 days of inactivity, with a floor of 100. This creates ongoing accountability, not
one-time qualification.

TEE integration: Baram's Nitro Enclave executor generates an RSA keypair inside the
enclave on startup. The private key never leaves the TEE. Prompts are encrypted with the
public key before transmission, decrypted inside the enclave, processed, and the result
returned with an NSM (Nitro Security Module) attestation signature. The attestation
chain is verified on-chain against registered PCR baselines.
